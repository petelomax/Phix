Once again, words fail me... pretty terrifying is about the best I can manage. Quite how anybody is ever expected to code correctly against that, well they just can't, can they? What that means to me is that beyond a certain size every Java program that has ever been or ever will be written is absolutely guaranteed to contain errors and omissions (and I know for a fact that is also true for C++). I say that not out of malice, but from a fundamental understanding that the probability is significantly less than flipping a coin a thousand times and getting heads every time, which is about 1 in 1e300, and for comparison there are only an estimated 1e80 subatomic particles in the entire universe, which has been around less than 5e17 seconds. To be fair, the odds of writing a perfectly correct Phix program (beyond a certain size) are pretty low too, but at least checking it for correctness is perhaps considerably closer to being plausibly possible, that is within a single lifetime.
