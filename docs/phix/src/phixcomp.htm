Compiling Phix

I once saw a post on a mailing list asking how to compile a simple C++ program, the answer given was: 
g++ -o sample_browser.exe ole_browser.cpp -I../../../include -L../../../lib/mingw6 -liupole -liup -lkernel32 -luser32 -lgdi32 -lwinspool -lcomdlg32 -ladvapi32 -lshell32 -lole32 -loleaut32 -luuid -lcomctl32
which struck me as not particularly unusual and somewhat harder to enter/remember than 
p -c ole_browser
Admittedly you might want "-norun" or "-d" and maybe "-dumpil" or "-nodiag" options, but that's it, relating to compilation.

The only other command line options are
"-p" [not intended for general use, windows-only create pw.exe from p.exe or vice versa],
"-batch" [suppress prompts on error, meant for test/terror],
"-isense" [an editor thing, for intellisense lookups], and
"-safe" [same as with safe_mode].
Oh, I suppose I should also mention "stub includes" such as builtins\theGUI.e which is just `include ..\demo\theGUI\theGUI.e` or a win32lib.ew containing (say) `include ..\win32lib7\win32lib.ew`.

In XE2, I kid thee not, there are a total of 35 screenfuls of build configuration options, times at least 6 levels of target hierarchy, plus 81 screens of IDE options, both of which are dwarfed by 70-odd properties and some 49 events on each and every gui component. Worst of all, there is no search or filter for any of that lot. At one point I estimated that meant there were over 45,000 places I had to check for something amiss (not that I'd recognise it right in front of my face) when some program or other I was working on was refusing to compile, and of course the error message was utterly indecipherable and gave "no suitable matches" in Google. Should you get anything wrong then consider yourself extremely lucky if you get any message at all from your average C++ compiler, even if total gibberish.

One other tale I like to tell is of the time when I performed a marathon ten day refactoring exercise on some C++ code. Every class needed dependency inversion, apparently (according to my boss) to better separate the business and display logic [how dare the display logic know it needs to display 4 columns, it should ask the business logic, and at the same time, how dare the business logic have the slightest clue what a column is...], and I could not think of any way to do that piecemeal. I think I broke OCP. Anyway, after two whole weeks without the slightest opportunity to compile/test anything, it came as little surprise that it refused to compile, with some truly baroque message (naturally one that Google had never heard of) about something deep in the compiler-provided standard files. It took a further three whole days to track down the single missing ')', and in fact I only found that because I was pasting code out of the C++ IDE into Edita, since that had (imnsho) a better file diff utility, and when I finally got to the offending source file (something like the 230th of 250) all it took was the paste operation, since straight away I could see that the final bracket was the wrong colour. Fifteen or so years later, it still astonishes me that C++ compiler cannot tell you when your brackets do not balance, including happily matching a closing '}' with an opening '(', ftloJ, and carries that imbalance over into the next source file.
