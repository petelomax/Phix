gRedraw

Definition: include theGUI.e

gRedraw(gdx id)
Description: Update the size and layout of all controls in the same dialog, and then, obviously, redraw them.

id: Identifier of the interface element.
pwa/p2js: Supported.
Notes: Redrawing too often can slow things down, however be advised that doing so at most once per second (and when the last is done) may make some program which actually stalls on the 2237th item appear to stall on some random item in the 21xx range, and no generic quick fix for that sort of thing springs to mind other than to put up with the slowdown while finding out precisely which item deserves deeper investigation.
See Also: gShow
Technicalia You may have to invoke this manually quite often, and wonder why (eg) gSetAttribute() does not do it automatically:

Suppose a gSplit has: 

    adj = left_panel_width - max(left_panel_width-adj,left_panel_min)
    left_panel_width -= adj
    right_panel_width += adj
    gSetAttribute(left_panel,"SIZE",{left_panel_width,left_panel_height})
    gSetAttribute(right_panel,"SIZE",{right_panel_width,right_panel_height})
    gRedraw(split)

The very last thing you would want is for the first size setting to shrink the entire window, before the second call restores it to the original size, and even if the flicker was barely noticeable, you certainly wouldn't want it to reposition everything outside the gSplit, twice, or for that matter be told about that, twice.

There were five similar routines in pGUI, namely IupRedraw(), IupRefresh(), IupRefreshChildren(), IupUpdate(), and IupUpdateChildren(), and I always ended up experimenting until finding the one (particular combination that) did precisely what I wanted.
The switch to fully emulated controls has (so far) made any such distinctions completely unnecessary.
