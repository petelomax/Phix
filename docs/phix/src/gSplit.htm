gSplit

Definition: include theGUI.e

gdx id = gSplit(gdx child1, child2, string orientation="HORIZONTAL")
Description: Creates a gH/Vbox with three children, the middle one being a (private) movable resizer, drawn as a dotted line.

child1/2: single element (eg a gCanvas) or nested container.
orientation: HORIZONTAL and VERTICAL refer to the container rather than the visible bar, see below.

Returns: the identifier of the created element.
pwa/p2js: Supported.
Example: 
-- demo\theGUI\gSplit.exw
include theGUI.e
procedure edge(gdx canvas, integer w,h)
    gDrawRect(canvas,1,1,w-2,h-2)
    integer cid = gGetInt(canvas,"USER_DATA")
    gDrawText(canvas,w/2,h/2,"%d",{cid},TG_C)
end procedure

gdx c1 = gCanvas(edge,`USER_DATA=1`),
    c2 = gCanvas(edge,`USER_DATA=2`), 
    c3 = gCanvas(edge,`USER_DATA=3`),
    vs = gSplit(c2,c3,"VERTICAL"),
    hs = gSplit(c1,vs),--,"HORIZONTAL"),
    dlg = gDialog(hs,"gSplit","SIZE=240x80")
gSetAttributes({c1,c2,c3},"BGCLR=PARCHMENT,FGCLR=OLIVE")
gMainLoop(dlg)
Notes: The whole point of a gSplit() is to draw, set the mouse pointer, and handle drag/drop of the separator.
It is just a gH/Vbox({child1,<private draggable canvas>,child2}) masquerading as a builtin/native control, but one that handles all the display, cursors, resizing, and mouse-dragging automatically for you.

The initial position is based on the initial natural/user sizes of the two children, both of which must of course be expandable in the appropriate direction(s), and is appropriately limited by their MIN/MAXSIZE.

The default orientation is HORIZONTAL, ie a gHbox() containing a vertical drag bar, with the two main halves side-by-side , as shown for '1'/pair above, whereas a VERTICAL orientation gets a gVbox() containing a horizontal drag bar, as shown for '2'/'3', with the two children stacked vertically. I suppose it would not be entirely unfair to say that HORIZONTAL is shorthand for HORIZONTAL_BOX_WITH_VERTICAL_SPLITTER, and I'll not bore you with the other one.
Attributes:
GAP,
MARGIN,
PADDING,
SPACE
 Not supported and in fact specifically prohibited.
A MARGIN could be specified on a parent container, perhaps one specifically introduced for that purpose, however that would probably lead to the visible splitter bar terminating "mid-air", which may look very odd, and since as noted above both child1 and 2 must be expandable, any SPACE setting would be ignored anyway. It is much more likely these attributes should be applied to gH/Vbox child1/2 instead, when desired that is.
ORIENTATION (read-only) inherited from the top-level gH/VBox.
